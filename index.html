The Adoption of Legalized Sports Betting across the United States.

Betting on sporting events is a controversial subject around the world and collectively represents a 150 billion dollar industry. In the United States, the industry has been growing and changing since the 1970's as as more and more states adopt new laws that allow betting on sports teams either in-person or online through regulated channels.

Historical Timeline

March 19, 1931

Nevada legalizes various forms of gambling. Although technically legal, betting on sports remained unregulated and was primarily the business of illegal bookies.

September 13, 1961

The Federal Wire Act was signed into law by President John F. Kennedy. The Federal Wire Act, also known as the Interstate Wire Act, is a federal law that prohibits the placing of wagers on any sporting event via the use of wire communications and also prohibits receipt of money via wire communications as a result of bets or wagers.

October 28, 1992

The Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA) was signed into law by President George H. W. Bush effectively making sports betting illegal in the United States. The law excluded Nevada. In addition, sports lotteries in Montana, Oregon and Delaware were exempt from the laws restricitions.

October 3, 2006

Coming into force as part of the SAFE Port Act, the Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act of 2006 (UIGEA) was signed into law by President George W. Bush. In practice, it simply made the movement of money into and out of gaming sites illegal, thus rendering it impossible to legally bet online within the US.
