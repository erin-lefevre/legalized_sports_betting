The Adoption of Legalized Sports Betting across the United States.

Up until several years ago, the only place in the United States where you could legally place a wager on a professional or college sporting event was in the state of Nevada.  A landmark Supreme Court decision in 2018 changed that.  As we approach the year 2021, betting on sporting events is now legal in over 20 states with more states currently on the path toward legalization.

Use the application below to learn about the history and the current status of legalized sports betting across the United States.

Historical Timeline

March 19, 1931

Nevada legalizes various forms of gambling and becomes the only state with legalized gambling.  However, betting on sports remained unregulated by the state and, as a result, sports betting remained an illegal, undeground activity run mostly by illegal bookies and semi-legal "turf clubs".

1949

Nevada sets regulatory framework for legalized sports betting and opens the first government-sanctioned sportsbook.  In addition, bookmakers across the state now had the permission and legal authority to accept bets on all professional sports as well as horse racing.

1951

The Federal Government imposes a 10% excise tax on all Nevada sportsbooks.  This ultimately drove the majority of sportsbooks underground and away from legitimate, regulated channels as legal sportsbooks could not compete with black market sports betting operations, many of which were run by the mob.

September 13, 1961

The Federal Wire Act was signed into law by President John F. Kennedy. The Federal Wire Act, also known as the Interstate Wire Act, is a federal law that prohibits the placing of wagers on any sporting event via the use of wire communications and also prohibits receipt of money via wire communications as a result of bets or wagers.

October 28, 1992

The Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA) was signed into law by President George H. W. Bush effectively making sports betting illegal in the United States. The law exempted Nevada. In addition, sports lotteries in Montana, Oregon and Delaware were exempt from the laws restricitions.

October 3, 2006

Coming into force as part of the SAFE Port Act, the Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act of 2006 (UIGEA) was signed into law by President George W. Bush. In practice, it simply made the movement of money into and out of gaming sites illegal, thus rendering it impossible to legally bet online within the US.

2011

New Jersey via voter referendum legalizes sports betting...

2012

Professional sports leagues and the NCAA sue to stop New Jersey legalized sports betting. Leads to eventual landmark supreme court case.

October 27, 2011

Nevada launches first legal online sports betting app.

May 14, 2018

SCOTUS rules PASPA Unconstitutional paving the way for states to take advantage of the 10th Amendment and legalize and regulate sports betting as they see fit.

May 3, 2019

Montana legalizes sports betting.

May 13, 2019

Iowa legalizes sports betting.

July 12, 2019

Sports betting legislation is passed in New Hampshire.

July 26, 2019

North Carolina legalizes sports betting at several tribal casinos within the state.

August 27, 2019

The first legal sports wagers are placed in Oregon.

November 6, 2019

Sports betting becomes legal in Colorado via referendum.

March, 2020

Sports betting commences at Illinois casinos.

March 25, 2020

Retail sports betting legalized at tribal casinos in the state of Washington.

April, 2020

Virgina signs sports betting bill into law. Expected launch date of both retail and online sports betting in early 2021.
